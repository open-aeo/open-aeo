The depth trap — paymoapp.com (17,893 words) Linear and Plane are being buried by a single exhaustive roundup. Pure content mass is winning even without schema markup, FAQs, or a comparison table. Neither brand has a page that competes on comprehensiveness.
secondtalent.com is the benchmark to beat The highest-signal competitor page in the set: FAQ section + FAQPage schema + ArticleSchema + 80 headings + 4,415 words + freshness signal. That combination is why it dominates the VS Code query over Codeium, Tabnine, and others.
Low bar for Docker / Next.js queries Fly.io and Neon/Firebase lost to pages with only ~1,260–1,457 words and no schema at all. A single well-structured native page would likely flip these results — these are the quickest wins available.
Schema adoption is near-zero across all competitors Only 1 of 10 competitor pages (secondtalent.com) uses FAQPage schema. Yet that same page is the strongest performer. Adding FAQPage + Article schema is a high-leverage, low-effort move none of the losing brands are using either.
